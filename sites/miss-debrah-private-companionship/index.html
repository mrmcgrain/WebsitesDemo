Client guide

How to make the first message easy to say yes to.

Drawn from provider etiquette resources: clear, courteous, and privacy-conscious.

Ask about fit, not entitlements

Private time is not a blank check. Ask what the experience includes, share your preferences respectfully, and accept boundaries without negotiation.

Lead with logistics

Include your name, desired date, duration, incall or outcall preference, and any timing constraints. Specific details reduce back-and-forth.

Discuss preferences with tact

Kinks or special requests belong in a respectful pre-booking conversation, not a demanding first line. Consent and comfort come first.

Be the easy regular

Punctuality, hygiene, kindness, privacy, and proper notice are remembered. Reliability is the fastest path to preferred-client treatment.

Policies

Professionalism is mutual.

No deposit is required. Keep booking simple by reaching out only when you are genuinely ready to reserve time.

Please provide proper notice for changes or cancellations. Last-minute changes make it harder to maintain a smooth, discreet schedule.

Privacy is expected on both sides. Do not share private details, images, or communications without consent.
