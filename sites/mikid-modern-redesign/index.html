About M.I.K.I.D.

The largest family-run behavioral health organization in Arizona.

M.I.K.I.D. is a non-profit, licensed outpatient clinic and family-run behavioral health organization serving children, youth, and families.

Through eight locations in Arizona, M.I.K.I.D. supports families with behavioral health, wellness, support, navigation, and family-centered care.

Over 80% of staff have lived experience as family members, parents, or young adults with behavioral health issues, and 51% of the Board of Directors have lived experience.

Three Key Components

Prevention, navigation, and reunification.

Services are organized around practical family support, youth programs, counseling, system navigation, and reunification assistance.

✦

Prevention

Helps strengthen families through interventions such as family support, counseling, and youth programs.

↗

Navigation

Assists families with navigating behavioral health systems, child-serving systems, and adult-serving systems, with support provided by individuals with lived experience.

●

Reunification

Provides reunification support through the ParentSupport Now program and helps families facing alternative dispositions.

Children's Mental Health

Children's mental disorders are an important public health issue in the United States.

It is estimated that 13%–20% of children living in the United States experience a mental disorder in a given year. Because of the impact on children, families, and communities, children's mental disorders are an important public health issue.
